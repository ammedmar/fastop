Examples and notebooks
======================

The notebooks are divided into introductory workflows and scientific case
studies.

Start here
----------

`Data structures and cohomology
<https://github.com/ammedmar/fastop/blob/main/notebooks/data_structures_and_cohomology.ipynb>`_
introduces the three input models, cohomology bases, cup products, and
Steenrod operations.

`Products, quotients, and symmetric powers
<https://github.com/ammedmar/fastop/blob/main/notebooks/products_quotients_and_symmetric_powers.ipynb>`_
explains normalized products, strict finite actions, direct symmetric-power
construction, and size preflighting.

Scientific examples
-------------------

`Steenrod operations on lens spaces
<https://github.com/ammedmar/fastop/blob/main/notebooks/lens_spaces.ipynb>`_
develops the join-quotient models used to detect Bocksteins and reduced
powers.

`Steenrod operations on symmetric products of surfaces
<https://github.com/ammedmar/fastop/blob/main/notebooks/symmetric_products_of_surfaces.ipynb>`_
compares the computed cohomology with the classical formulas and records the
prime-two, prime-three, prime-five, and prime-seven examples.

Cells marked with ``RUN_LARGE_*`` or a similar flag are intentionally
disabled by default.  They can require several minutes and more than a
gigabyte of memory.  The smaller introductory and prime-three examples are
the appropriate starting point.
